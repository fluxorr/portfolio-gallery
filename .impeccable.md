## Design Context

### Users
- **Primary**: Hiring Managers, CTOs, and Lead Design Engineers at high-growth tech companies (e.g., Vercel, Stripe, Linear).
- **Secondary**: Other developers and designers looking for technical inspiration.

### Brand Personality
- **Precision**: Pixel-perfect alignment and hyper-responsive feedback.
- **Mastery**: Demonstrating deep understanding of the "hidden" parts of the web (Performance, Accessibility, Math-based animation).
- **Pro**: Professional, stable, yet undeniably impressive.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual Tone**: High-contrast dark mode with focused cyan/indigo accents. 
- **Inspirations**: Stripe's technical documentation, Linear's interaction speed, Apple's hardware detail.
- **Anti-References**: Generic "bouncy" animations, slow transitions, over-the-top chitchat, or masking lack of substance with excessive particles.

### Design Principles
1. **Speed is the Feature**: Interactions must feel faster than the user expects. Use spring physics over easing.
2. **Contextual Continuity**: Elements should morph or flow between states rather than just appearing.
3. **Information Density with Clarity**: Technical data should be presented with high precision (tabular-nums, monospaced details).
4. **Accessible Overdrive**: Extreme effects must never compromise the ability to use the interface.
